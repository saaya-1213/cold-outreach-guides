How to Track Cold Email Replies Without a CRM

You don't need a $50/month CRM to know what's working. Updated 2026.

If you're sending fewer than a few hundred cold emails a week, a full CRM is overkill. You need three numbers: how many you sent, how many replied, and your reply rate. Here's the simplest way to track them.

The minimum you should track

FieldWhy it matters
Company / NameWho you contacted
Status (Sent / Replied)Where each lead stands
Sent dateTo time your one follow-up
Reply (yes/no)The number that tells you if it's working

The free spreadsheet method

Create columns for the fields above. After each send, set Status to "Sent" and the date. When someone replies, mark it. Your reply rate is simply replies ÷ sent. Review weekly: if you're under 5%, the problem is usually targeting or the personalization line, not the template.

What a good reply rate looks like

  • Under 3% — targeting or personalization is off. Tighten your list and your subject lines.
  • 5–10% — solid. Scale the version that's working.
  • 10%+ — strong product-market fit in your messaging. Don't change a thing; just send more.
