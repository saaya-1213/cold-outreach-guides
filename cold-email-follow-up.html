Cold Email Follow-Up: How Many and When

Most replies come after the first email. Here's how to follow up without being annoying.

A large share of cold email replies come from follow-ups, not the first touch — yet most people never send one. The trick is to add value or a new angle each time, not just "bumping" the thread.

How many follow-ups?

For most B2B outreach, 1–2 follow-ups is the sweet spot. Beyond that, returns drop fast and you risk annoyance. If two thoughtful follow-ups get nothing, move on.

Timing

  • Follow-up #1: 3–4 days after the first email.
  • Follow-up #2: 5–7 days after #1.
  • Send on weekday mornings in the recipient's timezone.

The rule that doubles replies

Never send "just bumping this." Each follow-up should carry one new thing: a different benefit, a quick proof point, or a genuinely easier ask.

Subject: last note, promise

Hi {name},
No worries if now's not the time. One thing I didn't mention:
{new angle / proof}. If it's useful I'll send it over — otherwise
I'll get out of your inbox.
{you}
