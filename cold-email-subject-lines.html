20 Cold Email Subject Lines That Get Replies (2026)

Short, specific, lowercase, no spam triggers. Updated 2026.

The subject line decides whether your cold email gets opened or ignored. After testing hundreds of B2B first-touch emails, the winners share four traits: under 5 words, one concrete detail, a question or curiosity gap, and zero spam words. Here are 20 you can copy today — swap { } for real details about the recipient.

The 20 subject lines

  1. quick idea for {company}
  2. {name}, saw your {trigger}
  3. re: your {product}
  4. 10 min this week?
  5. {company} + a quick thought
  6. one thing about {company}'s growth
  7. {name} — worth a quick look?
  8. cutting your outreach time
  9. for the {role} at {company}
  10. loved {trigger}
  11. before you buy another tool…
  12. your {product}, my 2 cents
  13. {name}, quick question
  14. helping {company}'s reply rate
  15. skip the CRM?
  16. last note, promise
  17. saw {trigger} — congrats
  18. a {role}-to-{role} question
  19. {company}: 30-second idea
  20. not a pitch, just curious

Why short subject lines win

Most inboxes truncate subjects after ~40 characters, and over half of B2B email is read on mobile where the cutoff is even shorter. A 3–4 word subject shows in full, reads like a note from a peer rather than a campaign, and avoids the pattern-matching that sends "FREE!! GUARANTEED $$$" straight to spam.

The personalization that actually matters

A subject line only works if the body proves you looked. That's the {trigger}: a recent funding round, a launch, a new hire, a specific product. One real detail beats ten merge tags. Keep the email to 50–70 words, make one point, and end with a soft ask ("worth a 10-minute look?").

Deliverability: stay out of spam

  • Ramp a new domain slowly (≤20/day the first week).
  • Set SPF, DKIM, and DMARC.
  • No links or images in email #1.
  • Avoid spam words (free, guarantee, $$$, !!!).
  • End with a question and always include an opt-out.
