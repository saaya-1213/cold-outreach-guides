What Is a Good Cold Email Reply Rate?

Benchmarks for 2026 and how to read yours.

Reply rate — not open rate — is the metric that matters for cold email. Open rates became unreliable after Apple and Google changed pixel tracking, but a reply is a reply. Here's what to aim for.

2026 reply rate benchmarks

Reply rateWhat it means
Under 3%Targeting or relevance is off. Fix the list and your opening line first.
3–5%Average. Workable, but there's upside in personalization.
5–10%Good. Scale the version that's working.
10%+Excellent — strong message-market fit. Just send more.

What moves the number

  • List quality — the #1 factor. 30 well-matched leads beat 3,000 random ones.
  • The relevance line — proof you looked (see the 4-line framework).
  • Deliverability — if you're in spam, your "reply rate" is really an inbox problem (checklist).
  • Follow-up — one polite follow-up often doubles total replies.

How to actually measure it

Reply rate = replies ÷ emails sent. Track it weekly so you can tell whether a change helped. You don't need a CRM for this — a simple tracker does the job.
