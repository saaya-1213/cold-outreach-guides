Reddit post draft (r/sales, r/Entrepreneur, r/coldemail)

Title: I tracked 127 cold outreach conversations — 3 small changes doubled my reply quality

Body:
I kept seeing "just send more volume" advice, so I tested the opposite:
quality-first messaging with strict constraints.

After 127 conversations, 3 changes made the biggest difference:

1) Context line < 14 words
   - Mention one real detail (role change, hiring post, product launch)
   - No fake personalization

2) Ask one low-friction question
   - Bad: "Can we book a call this week?"
   - Better: "Worth sending a 3-line teardown?"

3) Follow-up adds new value, not "just bumping"
   - Share one benchmark, one teardown, or one relevant template

LinkedIn post draft (founder/sales audience)

Most cold messages fail before line 2.

A simple fix that helped me:
use this structure for first touch.

[Context line]
Saw {specific trigger} and noticed {specific gap/opportunity}.

[Value line]
I made a tiny checklist/template for this exact case.

[Low-friction CTA]
Worth sending the 1-page version?

This format increased useful replies because it feels like a real human note,
not a sequence blast.
